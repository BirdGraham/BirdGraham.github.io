Question 1
Information and Ideas / Command of Evidence
In many high-altitude ecosystems, soil nutrient availability acts as a strict constraint on plant biomass. Ecologist Dr. Elena Vance hypothesized that specific subalpine cushion plants actively alter their immediate substrate chemistry, creating localized microenvironments that facilitate the survival of neighboring, less-hardy flora.
Which finding, if true, would most directly support Dr. Vance's hypothesis?
Correct Answer: B. The hypothesis states cushion plants actively alter substrate chemistry to help neighboring plants. Finding elevated bioavailable nitrogen directly beneath cushion plants confirms both the chemical alteration and its localized effect. The other choices address cushion plant growth (A), distant plant behavior (C), or genetics (D), none of which address the substrate chemistry claim.
Question 2
Craft and Structure / Words in Context
The corporate governance committee insisted that the executive's record, far from being completely unblemished, was actually marked by strategic omissions. Yet, because those omissions never technically violated local statutes, the final compliance report described his tenure as entirely _______.
Which choice completes the text with the most logical and precise word?
Correct Answer: A. The sentence establishes a contrast with "Yet": despite the omissions, they "never technically violated local statutes," so the report called his tenure lawful. "Licit" means lawful or permissible, a precise fit. "Peerless" means unmatched (doesn't fit the legal context), "anomalous" means irregular (contradicts the report's tone), and "explicit" means clearly stated (irrelevant here).
Question 3
Expression of Ideas / Transitions
Art historian Clara Simmons argues that mid-twentieth-century muralists were driven primarily by public accessibility, placing large-scale works in subways and public squares. _______, contemporary street artists often utilize identical urban surfaces but are frequently motivated by a desire to subvert commercial ownership of public visual spaces.
Which choice completes the text with the most logical transition?
Correct Answer: D. The two sentences describe artists who use the same surfaces but for opposing purposes: accessibility vs. subversion. This is a contrast relationship. "As a result" signals cause and effect, "In addition" signals continuation, "For instance" signals example. "By contrast" correctly signals a meaningful difference between the two groups.
Question 4
Craft and Structure / Text Structure and Purpose
Philosopher Nadia Osei opens her essay by describing a hypothetical city where every citizen receives identical resources from birth. She then systematically dismantles the assumption that identical inputs guarantee identical outcomes, citing variability in individual circumstance, preference, and capacity. The essay concludes by proposing a model centered on equity rather than equality.
Which choice best describes the overall structure of Osei's essay as presented in the passage?
Correct Answer: C. The passage maps three structural moves: (1) hypothetical city scenario, (2) dismantling the assumption that equal inputs equal equal outcomes, (3) a new proposal centered on equity. This matches "hypothetical scenario, critique of assumption, alternative proposal." The other choices misidentify the opening move (not personal anecdote or historical overview) or the closing move (not a call to action or reaffirmation).
Question 5
Information and Ideas / Central Ideas and Details
Marine biologist Dr. Tarek Nasser's long-term study of coral reef bleaching events found that reefs in areas with naturally variable water temperatures demonstrated markedly higher survival rates during extreme thermal events than reefs in areas of historically stable temperatures. Nasser attributed this pattern to thermal acclimatization, the gradual physiological adjustment that occurs when organisms are repeatedly exposed to stress within a survivable range.
Which statement best captures the main idea of the passage?
Correct Answer: B. The passage focuses on one finding: reefs with variable (stressful but survivable) temperature histories survived extreme heat better, explained by thermal acclimatization. Choice B captures this precisely. Choice A reverses the implication about stability. Choice C introduces reversibility, which the passage never claims. Choice D overstates the scope, since the passage is about survival rates, not biodiversity patterns.
Question 6
Standard English Conventions / Punctuation
Cognitive scientist Dr. Yuki Tanaka argues that multitasking is largely a cognitive illusion. What most people experience as simultaneous task execution is actually rapid task-switching _______ a process that carries measurable costs in both accuracy and reaction time.
Which punctuation mark most logically completes the sentence?
Correct Answer: A. The phrase "a process that carries measurable costs in both accuracy and reaction time" is an appositive. It renames and elaborates on "rapid task-switching." Appositives are set off by a comma. A semicolon (B) would require an independent clause after it, but the appositive is not one. A period (C) creates an incomplete sentence. No punctuation (D) creates a run-on structure.
Question 7
Expression of Ideas / Rhetorical Synthesis
A student is writing a report about urban heat islands and wants to use the following notes: Cities are typically 1 to 7 degrees Fahrenheit warmer than surrounding rural areas due to heat-absorbing surfaces like asphalt and concrete. Green roofs (rooftops covered with vegetation) can reduce building surface temperatures by up to 40 degrees. A 2021 study found that cities with 20% or more green roof coverage showed measurably lower ambient air temperatures. The student wants to emphasize the potential of green roofs as a solution to urban heat islands.
Which choice most effectively accomplishes this goal?
Correct Answer: C. The task is to emphasize green roofs as a solution. Choice C directly combines the two key data points, surface temperature reduction and city-wide cooling when widely adopted, into a solution-focused claim. Choice A only describes the problem. Choice B is vague and doesn't use the evidence. Choice D describes study methodology without drawing a conclusion about green roofs as a solution.
Question 8
Standard English Conventions / Form, Structure, and Sense
The research team, which had spent three years collecting behavioral data on migratory songbirds, _______ their findings at the annual ornithology symposium in April.
Which choice correctly completes the sentence?
Correct Answer: D. The time reference "in April" indicates a specific completed past event. Simple past tense ("presented") is correct. "Were presenting" (A) implies an ongoing action that was interrupted. "Have presented" (B) uses present perfect, which implies relevance to the present, not appropriate for a specific past date. "Are presenting" (C) is present tense, inconsistent with a completed April event.
Question 9
Craft and Structure / Cross-Text Connections
Text 1: Economist Priya Shah argues that universal basic income (UBI) would reduce financial anxiety, increase individual autonomy, and allow workers to pursue more meaningful employment without fear of poverty.

Text 2: Sociologist Marco Ferretti contends that cash transfer programs like UBI, while well-intentioned, risk undermining the psychological benefits derived from structured work, which provides social identity, routine, and a sense of contribution that money alone cannot replicate.
How would Ferretti most likely respond to Shah's claim that UBI allows workers to pursue more meaningful employment?
Correct Answer: B. Ferretti's core claim is that structured work provides identity, routine, and contribution that money can't replace. If UBI reduces the financial need to work, Ferretti would worry that people might work less, losing those psychological benefits. He wouldn't agree (A) because his concern is that financial security without work may not be better. He wouldn't fully support it (C) because he fears the structure of work disappears. He isn't indifferent (D) since his entire argument is about employment, not just income.
Question 10
Information and Ideas / Quantitative Evidence
A researcher studying reading speed and comprehension gave participants either printed texts or digital texts and measured both reading speed (words per minute) and comprehension accuracy (percent correct on a 10-question quiz). Results showed that participants reading printed texts averaged 238 wpm with 84% comprehension accuracy, while those reading digital texts averaged 267 wpm with 71% comprehension accuracy.
Which conclusion is best supported by the data in the passage?
Correct Answer: A. The data shows digital readers were faster (267 vs. 238 wpm) but had lower comprehension (71% vs. 84%). Choice A directly reflects both measurements without adding interpretation. Choice B introduces intelligence, which the data doesn't measure. Choice C makes a value judgment not supported by the data. Choice D contradicts the data, which shows comprehension did differ by medium.
Question 11
Craft and Structure / Words in Context
Though the senator's speech was praised by her supporters for its passion, political analysts noted that the argument was largely _______, relying on emotional appeals rather than verifiable data or logical structure.
Which choice completes the text with the most logical and precise word?
Correct Answer: C. The passage contrasts emotional appeals with "verifiable data or logical structure," suggesting the argument used persuasion techniques rather than evidence. "Rhetorical" precisely describes language designed to persuade through emotional effect rather than logic or data. "Cogent" means logically convincing (the opposite of what's described). "Fastidious" means extremely careful or particular (unrelated). "Empirical" means based on observation and data (also the opposite).
Question 12
Standard English Conventions / Sentence Boundaries
Architect Leila Moradi designed the community center to serve multiple functions _______ it houses a library on the ground floor, performance spaces on the second, and rooftop gardens accessible to all residents.
Which punctuation most effectively separates the two parts of this sentence?
Correct Answer: D. The second half of the sentence lists the specific functions, which directly explains and expands on "multiple functions." A colon is used to introduce a list or elaboration that follows a complete independent clause. A comma alone (A) would create a comma splice. No punctuation (B) produces a run-on. A period (C) would make the second part a fragment, since it lacks a main verb of its own.
Question 13
Information and Ideas / Command of Evidence (Textual)
Historian Dr. James Okoye argues that the decline of the Roman Republic was not caused by any single catastrophic event but rather by the gradual erosion of civic norms that had, for centuries, constrained the ambitions of individual leaders. He points to the precedent set by Sulla's march on Rome in 88 BCE as the decisive moment when it became imaginable for a Roman general to use his army against the state itself.
Which quotation from Okoye's work would most directly support his central argument?
Correct Answer: A. Okoye's argument has two parts: decline happened gradually through eroding norms, and Sulla's march made it imaginable to use armies against the state. Choice A directly connects both by saying Sulla's action dissolved the institutional taboo gradually over the following decades, linking the specific event to the gradual normative erosion. Choice B discusses military tactics, unrelated to the argument. Choice C focuses on the Senate's response, not norm erosion. Choice D makes a general claim about wealth, not the specific mechanism Okoye describes.
Question 14
Expression of Ideas / Effective Language Use
A student is writing an essay about the psychology of decision-making and wants to introduce the concept of "choice overload." The student wants to use precise academic language appropriate to the topic.
Which sentence best accomplishes this goal?
Correct Answer: B. Choice B uses precise academic language ("cognitive psychology," "paradoxically," "impair decision quality," "selected outcomes") and frames the concept in terms of research, appropriate for an academic essay. Choice A uses informal language ("really bad," "pick anything"). Choice C is vague and colloquial ("lots of choices," "well-known issue"). Choice D shifts the argument to consumer culture critique, which moves away from the psychological concept the student wants to introduce.
Question 15
Craft and Structure / Purpose of a Detail
Ornithologist Dr. Yemi Adeyemi's 2019 field study documented a species of weaver bird constructing nests using exclusively synthetic fibers, such as nylon string and plastic twine, in urban environments where natural nesting materials were scarce. Adeyemi noted that the finished nests were structurally indistinguishable from those built with traditional plant fibers.
Why does the author most likely include the detail about the finished nests being "structurally indistinguishable" from traditionally built nests?
Correct Answer: C. The detail that synthetic-material nests were structurally indistinguishable from traditional ones serves to show that the birds' adaptation to scarce natural materials was functionally successful. The nests still worked. Choice A overclaims (superiority is not established, only equivalence). Choice B introduces aesthetic preference, which is not supported by the passage. Choice D is too broad; the passage focuses on one species' nesting behavior, not wildlife in general.
Question 16
Standard English Conventions / Agreement
Each of the twelve panel members _______ required to submit a written summary of their findings within 48 hours of the final deliberation session.
Which choice correctly completes the sentence?
Correct Answer: D. The subject is "Each," which is grammatically singular even though it refers to twelve people. Singular indefinite pronouns like "each," "every," "either," and "neither" always take singular verbs. Therefore, "is" is correct. "Are" (A) and "were" (B) are plural. "Have been" (C) uses present perfect plural. This is one of the most frequently tested agreement rules on the SAT.
Question 17
Information and Ideas / Inferences
A 2022 analysis of urban food deserts found that neighborhoods with the fewest grocery stores per capita also had the highest rates of diet-related chronic illnesses. Researchers controlled for income, noting that even among residents with comparable earnings, those in areas with abundant grocery options consumed significantly more fresh produce than those in areas with limited options.
Which inference is most strongly supported by the passage?
Correct Answer: B. The key phrase is that researchers "controlled for income" and still found a difference in produce consumption based on grocery store availability. This means store access has an effect independent of income, directly supporting choice B. Choice A is contradicted by the study's design, which controlled for income to isolate store access as a variable. Choices C and D introduce explanations (culture, exercise) not present in the passage.
Question 18
Expression of Ideas / Transitions
Early aviation pioneers faced enormous skepticism from the scientific establishment. The Wright Brothers' initial flight at Kitty Hawk in 1903 was dismissed by several prominent engineers as a fluke. _______, within a decade, aviation technology had advanced to the point where powered flight was considered not only possible but commercially viable.
Which transition word or phrase best completes the passage?
Correct Answer: A. The sentence before the blank describes skepticism and dismissal. The sentence after describes rapid advancement and acceptance. The relationship is concessive: despite the skepticism, progress happened anyway. "Nevertheless" signals that something happened in spite of what came before, making it the precise fit. "Consequently" (B) would mean the skepticism caused the advancement, which is illogical. "Similarly" (C) would suggest a parallel, but the ideas contrast. "In other words" (D) signals restatement, not contrast.
Question 19
Craft and Structure / Author's Perspective
The proposed algorithm, its developers claimed, could detect fraud with 97% accuracy. What went unmentioned in the press release was the algorithm's false positive rate: in testing, it flagged 1 in 8 legitimate transactions as fraudulent. For a company processing 40 million transactions monthly, this would mean 5 million wrongful flags per month, each requiring human review.
Which best describes the author's perspective in this passage?
Correct Answer: C. The author highlights what "went unmentioned" in the press release, specifically the false positive rate, and then calculates its real-world impact to show how significant it is. This signals skepticism toward claims that highlight one impressive statistic (97% accuracy) while omitting a critical flaw. Choice A is the opposite of the author's stance. Choice B overstates uncertainty; the author critiques this specific claim, not all fraud detection technology. Choice D goes beyond what the passage states.
Question 20
Standard English Conventions / Modifiers
_______ for nearly a century, the abandoned lighthouse was finally restored by a team of local preservationists who spent three years on the project.
Which choice correctly completes the sentence?
Correct Answer: B. The introductory participial phrase must logically modify the subject of the main clause. The subject is "the abandoned lighthouse," and it makes sense for the lighthouse to have "stood empty for nearly a century." Choices C and D create dangling modifiers: "The preservationists standing empty" is nonsensical. A participial phrase at the start of a sentence must refer to the grammatical subject that immediately follows the comma.

Question 1
Advanced Math / Nonlinear Functions
A bacterial population triples in size every 4 hours. If the initial population at \(t = 0\) is 150, which expression represents the population after \(t\) hours?
Correct Answer: C. Exponential growth follows \(P = I \cdot R^{t/p}\), where \(I = 150\) (initial value), \(R = 3\) (tripling factor), and \(p = 4\) (period in hours). The exponent \(t/4\) counts how many 4-hour periods have passed. After 4 hours: \(150 \cdot 3^{4/4} = 150 \cdot 3 = 450\). Choice A makes the exponent \(4t\), which would give \(3^{4}\) after just 1 hour, far too fast.
Question 2
Algebra / Linear Equations in One Variable
A catering company charges a flat setup fee of $85 plus $24 per guest. If a client's total bill before tax was $1,165, how many guests attended?
Correct Answer: B. Set up: \(24g + 85 = 1{,}165\). Subtract 85: \(24g = 1{,}080\). Divide by 24: \(g = 45\). Check: \(24(45) + 85 = 1{,}080 + 85 = 1{,}165\). Correct!
Question 3
Advanced Math / Systems of Equations
In the \(xy\)-plane, the parabola \(y = x^2 - 6x + 14\) intersects the line \(y = 2x + k\) at exactly one point. What is the value of \(k\)?
Correct Answer: A. Set equal: \(x^2 - 6x + 14 = 2x + k\), giving \(x^2 - 8x + (14 - k) = 0\). For exactly one intersection, the discriminant equals zero: \((-8)^2 - 4(1)(14-k) = 0 \Rightarrow 64 - 56 + 4k = 0 \Rightarrow 8 + 4k = 0 \Rightarrow k = -2\).
Question 4
Problem Solving and Data Analysis / Ratios and Proportions
A map uses a scale of 1 inch = 35 miles. Two cities are 4.6 inches apart on the map. What is the actual distance, in miles, between the two cities?
Correct Answer: D. Distance = \(4.6 \times 35 = 161\) miles. Always identify exactly what is being asked before calculating, and match units carefully throughout.
Question 5
Algebra / Linear Functions
A line passes through the points \((3,\ 11)\) and \((-1,\ -5)\). Which of the following is the equation of this line?
Correct Answer: C. Slope \(m = \dfrac{11-(-5)}{3-(-1)} = \dfrac{16}{4} = 4\). Using point-slope with \((3, 11)\): \(y - 11 = 4(x - 3) \Rightarrow y = 4x - 12 + 11 \Rightarrow y = 4x - 1\). Verify with \((-1, -5)\): \(4(-1) - 1 = -5\). Correct!
Question 6
Advanced Math / Polynomial Expressions
Which expression is equivalent to \((2x^3 - 5x^2 + 3x - 7) - (x^3 + 2x^2 - 6x + 4)\)?
Correct Answer: B. Distribute the negative: \(2x^3 - 5x^2 + 3x - 7 - x^3 - 2x^2 + 6x - 4\). Combine like terms: \(x^3 - 7x^2 + 9x - 11\). The most common error is forgetting to distribute the negative sign to ALL terms in the second polynomial.
Question 7
Geometry and Trigonometry / Area and Volume
A cylindrical water tank has a radius of 6 feet and a height of 10 feet. If water is filling the tank at a rate of \(9\pi\) cubic feet per minute, how many minutes will it take to fill the tank completely from empty?
Correct Answer: A. Volume of cylinder \(= \pi r^2 h = \pi (6)^2 (10) = 360\pi\) cubic feet. Time \(= \dfrac{360\pi}{9\pi} = 40\) minutes. Note that \(\pi\) cancels cleanly, a common SAT design feature.
Question 8
Problem Solving and Data Analysis / Statistics
A data set of 6 values has a mean of 14. If one value of 8 is removed, what is the mean of the remaining 5 values?
Correct Answer: D. Total sum \(= 6 \times 14 = 84\). Remove 8: new sum \(= 76\). New mean \(= \dfrac{76}{5} = 15.2\). Always adjust both the sum AND the count when removing a value.
Question 9
Advanced Math / Exponential and Radical Functions
Which value of \(x\) satisfies the equation \(\sqrt{3x + 13} = x + 3\)?
Correct Answer: B. Square both sides: \(3x + 13 = x^2 + 6x + 9\). Rearrange: \(x^2 + 3x - 4 = 0 \Rightarrow (x+4)(x-1) = 0\). Check \(x = -4\): \(\sqrt{1} = 1\) but \(-4+3 = -1\). Extraneous! Check \(x = 1\): \(\sqrt{16} = 4\) and \(1+3 = 4\). Always check for extraneous solutions when squaring both sides.
Question 10
Geometry and Trigonometry / Trigonometric Ratios
In a right triangle, the angle \(\theta\) satisfies \(\sin\theta = \dfrac{5}{13}\). What is the value of \(\cos\theta\)?
Correct Answer: C. Opposite = 5, hypotenuse = 13. Adjacent \(= \sqrt{13^2 - 5^2} = \sqrt{144} = 12\). Therefore \(\cos\theta = \dfrac{12}{13}\). This is the classic 5-12-13 Pythagorean triple, worth memorizing for the SAT.
Question 11
Algebra / Systems of Linear Equations
If \(2x + 3y = 17\) and \(4x - y = 11\), what is the value of \(x + y\)?
Correct Answer: A. Multiply the second equation by 3: \(12x - 3y = 33\). Add to the first: \(14x = 50\), so \(x = \dfrac{50}{14} = \dfrac{25}{7}\). Substitute back: \(4\left(\dfrac{25}{7}\right) - y = 11 \Rightarrow y = \dfrac{100}{7} - 11 = \dfrac{17}{7}\). Therefore \(x + y = \dfrac{25}{7} + \dfrac{17}{7} = \dfrac{42}{7} = 6\).
Question 12
Advanced Math / Quadratic Equations
The function \(f(x) = x^2 - 10x + 21\) has two zeros. What is the sum of those zeros?
Correct Answer: D. By Vieta's formulas, for a quadratic \(ax^2 + bx + c\), the sum of the roots equals \(-b/a\). Here, \(a = 1\) and \(b = -10\), so the sum is \(-(-10)/1 = 10\). You can also factor: \((x-3)(x-7) = 0\) gives roots 3 and 7, and \(3 + 7 = 10\). Knowing Vieta's formulas saves significant time on the SAT.
Question 13
Problem Solving and Data Analysis / Probability
A bag contains 4 red marbles, 6 blue marbles, and 2 green marbles. If one marble is drawn at random, what is the probability that it is NOT green?
Correct Answer: B. Total marbles = 12. Green marbles = 2. Probability of green = \(\dfrac{2}{12} = \dfrac{1}{6}\). Probability of NOT green = \(1 - \dfrac{1}{6} = \dfrac{5}{6}\). Always check whether the question asks for the probability of an event or its complement, since the SAT frequently asks for the complement.
Question 14
Advanced Math / Rational Expressions
Which expression is equivalent to \(\dfrac{x^2 - 9}{x^2 - x - 6}\) for all values of \(x\) where the expression is defined?
Correct Answer: C. Factor numerator: \(x^2 - 9 = (x+3)(x-3)\). Factor denominator: \(x^2 - x - 6 = (x-3)(x+2)\). Cancel the common factor \((x-3)\): result is \(\dfrac{x+3}{x+2}\). Note that the expression is undefined when \(x = 3\) or \(x = -2\) (the original denominator's zeros). Always factor fully before canceling.
Question 15
Geometry and Trigonometry / Circles
A circle has equation \(x^2 + y^2 - 8x + 6y = 0\). What is the radius of this circle?
Correct Answer: A. Complete the square: \((x^2 - 8x + 16) + (y^2 + 6y + 9) = 0 + 16 + 9\). This gives \((x-4)^2 + (y+3)^2 = 25\). The radius is \(\sqrt{25} = 5\). Center is at \((4, -3)\). When completing the square, remember to add the same values to both sides of the equation.
Question 16
Problem Solving and Data Analysis / Percentages
A store marks up wholesale items by 40% to set the retail price. During a sale, the retail price is discounted by 25%. The final sale price is what percent of the original wholesale price?
Correct Answer: D. Let wholesale = 100. Retail = \(100 \times 1.40 = 140\). Sale price = \(140 \times 0.75 = 105\). So the final price is 105% of wholesale. A common error is subtracting 40% and 25% directly (which gives 15% net change). Percent changes must be applied sequentially, not added.
Question 17
Algebra / Inequalities
Which of the following represents all values of \(x\) that satisfy \(|2x - 6| \leq 10\)?
Correct Answer: B. For \(|2x - 6| \leq 10\), split into: \(-10 \leq 2x - 6 \leq 10\). Add 6: \(-4 \leq 2x \leq 16\). Divide by 2: \(-2 \leq x \leq 8\). For absolute value inequalities: if \(|A| \leq k\), the solution is \(-k \leq A \leq k\) (a closed interval). If \(|A| \geq k\), the solution is \(A \leq -k\) or \(A \geq k\) (two separate rays).
Question 18
Advanced Math / Function Notation and Transformations
If \(f(x) = 2x^2 + 3\), what is \(f(x + 1) - f(x)\)?
Correct Answer: C. \(f(x+1) = 2(x+1)^2 + 3 = 2(x^2 + 2x + 1) + 3 = 2x^2 + 4x + 2 + 3 = 2x^2 + 4x + 5\). Then \(f(x+1) - f(x) = (2x^2 + 4x + 5) - (2x^2 + 3) = 4x + 2\). The \(2x^2\) and constant 3 cancel, leaving only the linear terms. This type of "difference of function values" question appears frequently on the SAT.
Question 19
Problem Solving and Data Analysis / Data Interpretation
A survey of 200 high school students found that 120 play a sport, 90 play an instrument, and 40 play both a sport and an instrument. How many students play neither a sport nor an instrument?
Correct Answer: A. Using the inclusion-exclusion principle: students who play a sport OR an instrument = \(120 + 90 - 40 = 170\). Students who play neither = \(200 - 170 = 30\). A common error is forgetting to subtract the overlap (the 40 students who do both), which would lead to double-counting them.
Question 20
Advanced Math / Equivalent Forms of Expressions
Which expression is equivalent to \(\dfrac{3}{x-2} + \dfrac{5}{x+4}\)?
Correct Answer: D. Common denominator is \((x-2)(x+4)\). Rewrite: \(\dfrac{3(x+4) + 5(x-2)}{(x-2)(x+4)} = \dfrac{3x + 12 + 5x - 10}{(x-2)(x+4)} = \dfrac{8x + 2}{(x-2)(x+4)}\). When adding rational expressions, always find the LCD first, then combine numerators carefully, distributing signs throughout.
