Question 1
Information and Ideas / Command of Evidence
In many high-altitude ecosystems, soil nutrient availability acts as a strict constraint on plant biomass. Ecologist Dr. Elena Vance hypothesized that specific subalpine cushion plants actively alter their immediate substrate chemistry, creating localized microenvironments that facilitate the survival of neighboring, less-hardy flora.
Which finding, if true, would most directly support Dr. Vance’s hypothesis?
Correct Answer: B. Dr. Vance's hypothesis hinges entirely on cushion plants actively changing their substrate chemistry to help nearby plants. Showing that soil directly underneath these plants contains higher bioavailable nitrogen (a nutrient) explicitly confirms this chemical modification.
Question 2
Craft and Structure / Words in Context
The corporate governance committee insisted that the executive's record, far from being completely unblemished, was actually marked by strategic omissions. Yet, because those omissions never technically violated local statutes, the final compliance report described his tenure as entirely _______.
Which choice completes the text with the most logical and precise word?
Correct Answer: A. The sentence establishes a contrast using "Yet." Even though there were omissions, they "never technically violated local statutes." Therefore, the report must describe his tenure as legal or permitted by law. "Licit" directly means lawful or legal.
Question 3
Expression of Ideas / Transitions
Art historian Clara Simmons argues that mid-twentieth-century muralists were driven primarily by public accessibility, placing large-scale works in subways and public squares. _______, contemporary street artists often utilize identical urban surfaces but are frequently motivated by a desire to subvert commercial ownership of public visual spaces.
Which choice completes the text with the most logical transition?
Correct Answer: D. The passage compares two eras of artists using the same spaces but for different core reasons: mid-century muralists wanted "public accessibility," while contemporary street artists want to "subvert commercial ownership." This is a clear contrast, making "By contrast" the correct fit.

A common assumption has been that calcite crystallization occurs in isotopic equilibrium with dripping water. However, meteorologist Marcus Thorne demonstrated that rapid kinetics and localized kinetic evaporation within arid cave mouths distort these parameters, rendering adjacent formations unreliable barometers of regional rainfall intensity.
Based on the passage, why might some cave speleothems yield inaccurate paleoclimate records?

• Automated power looms dramatically minimized production costs and accelerated output times.

The student wants to contrast the primary advantage of handloom weaving with the primary advantage of automated power looms. Which choice most effectively uses information from the given notes to accomplish this goal?

Question 7
Information and Ideas / Textual Evidence
Biologist Dr. Kenji Sato monitored marine top predator behaviors surrounding deep hydrothermal vent structures. Standard ecological paradigms suggest that apex species restrict tracking movements to shallow, photic zones due to acute pressure thresholds. Dr. Sato observed, however, that specific sub-populations of swordfish regularly initiate vertical foraging dives extending well into aphotic bathypelagic layers. He contends that extreme ambient warmth leaking from oceanic rifts offsets the metabolic costs of hunting in freezing, deep-sea currents.
Which observation, if true, would most directly undermine Dr. Sato’s dynamic contention?
Correct Answer: D. Dr. Sato's contention rests specifically on the idea that *thermal heat* from the vents allows the dives. If swordfish dive just as often in cold rifts with no thermal heat, it disproves that thermal heat is the driving metabolic factor.

For decades, economists assumed that urban expansion universally diminishes local biodiversity by fragmenting contiguous natural corridors. Text 2
Ecologist Nia Brooks cautions against overestimating the conservation utility of urban greening projects. The concentrated presence of household domestic predators and acute noise pollution regularly disrupt avian nesting behaviors, leading to net negative fledgling survival metrics compared to natural forest interiors.

Question 1
Advanced Math / Nonlinear Functions
A bacterial population triples in size every 4 hours. If the initial population size at t = 0 is 150, which of the following expressions represents the total population size after t hours?
Correct Answer: C. Exponential growth is modeled by the format P = I(R)^(t/p), where I is the initial value (150), R is the rate multiplier (3), and p is the interval period (4 hours). Therefore, the correct exponent tracking the total hours step-by-step is t/4.
Question 2
Algebra / Linear Equations
A local catering company charges a flat setup fee of 85 dollars plus an additional 24 dollars per guest. If a client's total bill before tax was 1165 dollars, how many guests attended the event?
Correct Answer: B. Set up the linear equation: 24g + 85 = 1165. Subtracting 85 from both sides gives 24g = 1080. Dividing both sides by 24 gives g = 45 guests.
Question 3
Advanced Math / Quadratic Systems
In the xy-plane, a parabola with the equation y = x^2 - 6x + 14 intersects a linear line given by y = 2x + k at exactly one point. What is the value of the constant k?
Correct Answer: A. Set equations equal: x^2 - 6x + 14 = 2x + k -> x^2 - 8x + (14 - k) = 0. For exactly one intersection point, the discriminant must equal zero: b^2 - 4ac = 0. Thus, (-8)^2 - 4(1)(14 - k) = 0 -> 64 - 56 + 4k = 0 -> 8 + 4k = 0 -> k = -2.
Question 4
Problem Solving and Data Analysis / Statistics
A sample of 400 residential homes in a city had a mean monthly electricity consumption of 840 kilowatt-hours (kWh), with an associated margin of error of 35 kWh at a 95% confidence level. Which of the following statements is a valid conclusion based on these parameters?
Correct Answer: D. Statistical confidence intervals estimate population parameters, meaning it is plausible that the true population mean falls within the calculated margin range (840 ± 35, which spans 805 to 875). It does not guarantee values for individual homes or state exact certainties.
Question 5
Geometry and Trigonometry / Circles
In the xy-plane, the equation of a circle is given by x^2 + y^2 - 10x + 4y = 7. What is the radius of this circle?
Correct Answer: B. Group the terms to complete the square: (x^2 - 10x + 25) + (y^2 + 4y + 4) = 7 + 25 + 4. This simplifies to standard circle form: (x - 5)^2 + (y + 2)^2 = 36. Since r^2 = 36, the radius r = 6.
Question 6
Algebra / Systems of Inequalities
If a system of linear inequalities is defined by y ≥ 3x - 4 and 2x + 3y < 12, which of the following coordinate ordered pairs (x, y) represents a valid solution in the solution set?
Correct Answer: A. Substitute (1, 2): First check 2 ≥ 3(1) - 4 -> 2 ≥ -1 (True). Next check 2(1) + 3(2) < 12 -> 2 + 6 < 12 -> 8 < 12 (True). Both conditions match perfectly.
Question 7
Advanced Math / Rational Equations
Given the expression (4x + 12) / (x^2 - 9) * (x^2 - 2x - 3) / (8x + 8) for all x > 3, which of the following represents its completely simplified form?
Correct Answer: D. Factor every single term: [4(x + 3)] / [(x - 3)(x + 3)] * [(x - 3)(x + 1)] / [8(x + 1)]. Cancel out common matching binomial blocks across fractions leaves: 4 / 8, which simplifies directly down to 1/2.
Question 8
Problem Solving and Data Analysis / Rates and Ratios
A standard processing plant line converts chemical raw materials at a continuous rate of 12 liters every 45 seconds. At this exact steady conversion rate, how many total kiloliters (kL) of materials will the facility process over an operating window of 5 complete hours? (Note: 1 kiloliter = 1,000 liters)
Correct Answer: B. Find output per minute: (12 L / 45 sec) * 60 sec = 16 L per minute. For 5 hours (300 minutes): 16 L * 300 = 4,800 total Liters. Convert to kiloliters: 4,800 / 1,000 = 4.8 kL.
Question 9
Geometry and Trigonometry / Right Triangles
In a right-angled triangle ABC, angle B measures exactly 90 degrees. If the value of cos(A) = 7/25, what is the value of tan(C)?
Correct Answer: A. If cos(A) = adjacent/hypotenuse = 7/25, then using the Pythagorean theorem (7-24-25 triplet), the remaining opposite side to angle A is 24. For angle C, side 7 becomes the opposite and side 24 becomes the adjacent. Thus, tan(C) = opposite/adjacent = 7/24.
Question 10
Algebra / Constants and Linear Functions
The linear function f is defined by f(x) = ax + b, where a and b are real constants. If f(2) = 11 and f(5) = 26, what is the value of f(-3)?
Correct Answer: C. Find the slope (constant a): (26 - 11) / (5 - 2) = 15 / 3 = 5. Now find intercept b: 11 = 5(2) + b -> b = 1. The full function line structure is f(x) = 5x + 1. Evaluating for f(-3) yields 5(-3) + 1 = -15 + 1 = -14.
